RSI — Reading Momentum

The RSI (Relative Strength Index) is the most widely used momentum oscillator in the world. When used correctly — not as an overbought/oversold trigger but as a momentum and divergence tool — it dramatically improves the quality of your entry and exit decisions.

What RSI Measures

The Relative Strength Index (RSI), developed by J. Welles Wilder, measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes to assess whether a security is gaining momentum or losing it. It oscillates between 0 and 100.

RSI compares the average gains to average losses over a lookback period (default: 14). If Nifty has been closing up more days than down over the past 14 sessions, RSI will be above 50. If it has been closing down more, RSI will be below 50.

📌

RSI formula summary: RS = Average Gain (14 periods) ÷ Average Loss (14 periods). RSI = 100 − (100 ÷ (1 + RS)). When RS is infinite (only gains, no losses), RSI = 100. When RS = 0 (only losses), RSI = 0. In practice, RSI stays mostly between 20 and 80.

3

The Biggest RSI Mistake

The most common (and most costly) RSI mistake: shorting when RSI crosses above 70, and buying when RSI crosses below 30. This is the exact opposite of what RSI was designed for — and it causes traders to fight strong trends.

Nifty RSI Overbought Trap — Jan–Mar 2024
Nifty's RSI (14-day) crossed above 70 in late January 2024 when Nifty was at 21,700. By the textbook "overbought = sell" rule, traders shorted here. Nifty continued to 22,526 — rising another 826 points while the RSI stayed above 70 for nearly 3 weeks. Every short was stopped out. RSI above 70 in a strong bull trend means MOMENTUM IS HIGH — not that a reversal is imminent.
⚠️

The correct interpretation: RSI above 70 in a bull trend = strong momentum, trend continuation likely. Only use RSI levels as reversal signals when combined with a price reversal at a key level AND RSI divergence (covered in the next two sections). Standalone RSI levels are NOT trade signals.

4

RSI Divergence — Bullish

Bullish RSI divergence occurs when price makes a lower low but RSI makes a higher low. This means the downward momentum is weakening even as price falls further — sellers are running out of energy. It's an early warning that a reversal may be near.

1
Price makes Low 1, RSI registers a low

Note the RSI value at the first significant price low. This is your reference point.

2
Price rallies partially then makes Low 2 (lower than Low 1)

Price makes a new lower low — bearish from a price action perspective. However, you now check RSI...

3
RSI at Low 2 is HIGHER than at Low 1 = bullish divergence confirmed

Despite price making a new low, RSI is showing less bearish momentum. This is the divergence. It signals that sellers are exhausted — the bears couldn't push RSI to new lows even though price went lower.

4
Wait for a reversal candle at the price low for entry

Divergence alone is not an entry. Wait for a Hammer, Bullish Engulfing, or Morning Star at the price low. Divergence + reversal candle + key S/R level = high-probability long setup.

Nifty Bullish RSI Divergence — Oct 2023
Low 1 (Oct 4): Nifty at 19,251, RSI(14) = 38.
Low 2 (Oct 26): Nifty at 18,837 (new lower low), RSI(14) = 44 (higher than 38).

Price made a lower low. RSI made a higher low. Classic bullish divergence at the 200 EMA. Combined with the Bullish Engulfing candle at the 200 EMA (covered in Chapter 7), this was one of the highest-probability long setups of 2023. Nifty ran from 18,837 to over 21,000 within 6 weeks.
5

RSI Divergence — Bearish

Bearish RSI divergence is the exact mirror: price makes a higher high but RSI makes a lower high. Buying pressure is declining even as price continues higher — a warning that the trend is exhausting.

Nifty Bearish RSI Divergence — ATH Zone Sept 2024
High 1 (Sept 20): Nifty at 25,383, RSI(14) = 68.
High 2 (Sept 26): Nifty at 25,670 (new ATH), RSI(14) = 62 (lower than 68).

Price made a new ATH. RSI made a lower high. Bearish divergence at the ATH zone. Combined with the near-Gravestone Doji candle at the high (covered in Chapter 2), this was the signal to close longs and prepare for a correction. Nifty fell to 23,893 over the following 3 weeks — a 7% correction.
⚠️

Divergence timing: RSI divergence can play out over varying timeframes. A daily chart divergence may take 1–3 weeks to fully materialise. A 15-min chart divergence may take 1–4 hours. Never size up aggressively on divergence alone — trade it with reduced size and tight stops, adding only as the reversal confirms.

6

RSI Midline (50) — The Bias Filter

The RSI 50 level is the neutral line — the midpoint between overbought and oversold. Crossing above 50 indicates that recent gains are outpacing losses (bullish momentum). Crossing below 50 indicates losses outpacing gains (bearish momentum).

Using RSI 50 as a bias filter improves trade quality significantly:

Bull
Only take long trades when RSI is above 50

RSI above 50 means the recent price momentum is bullish. Long trades in this zone align with momentum. Below 50 = the momentum is working against your long — avoid new longs.

Bear
Only take short trades when RSI is below 50

RSI below 50 means recent momentum is bearish. Short trades in this zone have momentum on their side. Above 50 = momentum is turning bullish — avoid new shorts or tighten stops on existing ones.

💡

RSI 50 crossover as trend confirmation: When RSI crosses above 50 after being below it for weeks, this often coincides with an EMA crossover or CPR bullish breakout — multiple indicators confirming the same trend change at the same time. These "stack" confirmations are the highest-conviction entries.

RSI Trend Following — Bull/Bear Zones

In strong trending markets, RSI tends to range within specific zones rather than oscillating between overbought and oversold. Recognising these zones lets you trade with the trend instead of against it.

Market PhaseRSI Typical RangeImplication
Strong bull trend40–80 (bull zone)RSI pulls back to 40–50 on dips (buy zone) and rallies to 70–80 on advances
Ranging market30–70 (full oscillation)RSI uses the full range — buy near 30, sell near 70
Strong bear trend20–60 (bear zone)RSI rallies to 50–60 (short zone) and falls to 20–30 on declines
💡

Practical application: In Nifty's 2023–2024 bull market, RSI regularly dipped to 40–45 during corrections before recovering. Traders who bought every RSI 40–45 touch on the daily chart (in alignment with the bullish EMA stack) captured the best risk/reward entries of the entire bull run.

8

RSI Failure Swings

An RSI Failure Swing is a specific pattern within the RSI line itself that signals a reversal — independent of price action. It is considered one of the most reliable RSI signals because it doesn't depend on comparing price to RSI (divergence) but instead reads the RSI's own momentum.

Bearish Failure Swing

1
RSI rises above 70 (overbought)

Momentum is strong — RSI reaches overbought territory.

2
RSI pulls back below 70

A minor RSI correction occurs — it briefly dips back below the 70 level.

3
RSI rallies again but FAILS to reach 70 again

This is the key signal — RSI made a lower high while price potentially made a new high. Momentum is clearly waning.

4
RSI breaks below the prior pullback low = SELL signal

When RSI breaks below the low of the pullback made in Step 2, the failure swing is confirmed. This is the short entry trigger.

The bullish failure swing is the exact mirror: RSI drops below 30, bounces, tries to go lower but fails (higher low in RSI), then breaks above the prior bounce high = buy signal.

Best RSI Settings for Nifty

The default RSI period (14) works well for daily charts. For intraday Nifty trading, different period settings may give cleaner signals:

Chart TimeframeRecommended RSI PeriodWhy
5-min intraday9 or 14Faster RSI captures intraday momentum shifts better. 14 is smoother, 9 is more responsive (more signals, more noise).
15-min intraday14 (default)The standard 14-period works well. Provides a good balance of responsiveness and smoothness.
Daily chart14 (default)Wilder's original setting is optimal for daily chart analysis in all markets including Nifty.
Weekly chart14Same default. Weekly RSI divergence is very powerful — use for identifying major turning points.
✅

The RSI sweet spot for divergence: For identifying divergence on the daily chart, use RSI(14). For intraday divergence on 15-min chart, RSI(14) also works well. The key is consistency — use the same settings every day so you can visually recognise patterns quickly without recalibrating.

Common Mistakes & Key Takeaways

📉
Shorting RSI Above 70
The #1 RSI mistake — treating overbought as a sell signal. In a bull trend, RSI stays above 70 for extended periods. Every short is stopped out repeatedly.
Fix: RSI above 70 in a bull trend = momentum is high, not a sell. Only short at RSI 70+ if you also have: (1) bearish divergence AND (2) a reversal candle at resistance.
⏰
Acting on Divergence Too Early
Entering a reversal trade the moment divergence is spotted without waiting for a confirming candle or price pattern. Divergence can develop over many more periods before the reversal.
Fix: Treat divergence as a yellow light, not a green light. Wait for a reversal candle at a key S/R level AFTER spotting divergence before entering.
🔢
Changing RSI Settings Constantly
Backtesting different RSI periods until finding one that "perfectly" caught historical tops/bottoms. This is curve fitting — the optimised settings won't work going forward.
Fix: Use RSI(14) universally. The standard settings work because enough other traders use them — creating self-fulfilling reactions at key RSI levels.
🔍
Ignoring RSI 50 as a Filter
Taking long trades when RSI is at 35–40 (momentum still bearish) because "it looks oversold." Fighting bearish momentum on longs leads to slow, losing trades even when price eventually works.
Fix: For directional trades, require RSI above 50 for longs, below 50 for shorts. This single filter eliminates many poor timing decisions.
📊
Watching RSI Alone
Making trading decisions based solely on RSI without looking at price, EMAs, or S/R levels. RSI is a confirmation and filter tool — not a complete trading system.
Fix: Always use RSI as one factor in a 3-confluence rule: price at key level + reversal candle + RSI confirmation. All three together = trade. One alone = no trade.
🔄
Missing Hidden Divergence
Only looking for regular divergence (trend reversals) and missing hidden divergence — where price makes a higher low but RSI makes a lower low. Hidden bullish divergence is a trend continuation signal, not reversal.
Fix: Learn both regular (reversal) and hidden (continuation) divergence. In strong trends, hidden divergence occurs during pullbacks — confirming the trend will resume.

✓Bullish RSI divergence (price lower low, RSI higher low) signals exhausted selling — wait for a reversal candle to confirm entry
✓Bearish RSI divergence (price higher high, RSI lower high) signals weakening buying — wait for a reversal candle at resistance to confirm short
