Volume Analysis

Price tells you what happened. Volume tells you how seriously it happened. A breakout on high volume is conviction. The same breakout on low volume is a trap. Learn to read volume as the most important confirmation tool in your technical analysis toolkit.

Volume — The Market's Lie Detector

Every price move is accompanied by volume — the number of shares/lots traded in that period. Volume reveals the participation level behind any price move. Price can be manipulated by a few large orders. Volume is much harder to fake because it requires actual capital to be committed.

When institutional players — foreign institutional investors (FIIs), domestic mutual funds, large prop desks — are actively participating, volume is high. When only retail traders are pushing price around, volume is low. Learning to read this is like having a window into what the "smart money" is doing.

📌

Core rule: High volume confirms moves. Low volume suspects them. A price move of 200 points on 3× average volume = real move. The same 200 points on 0.5× volume = be suspicious, it may reverse.

What Volume Measures in Indian Markets

In Nifty futures (NSE), volume is measured in lots transacted. In spot stocks (NSE/BSE), it is in shares. For equity charts, most charting platforms show the total shares traded per candle. The absolute number matters less than the comparison to recent average volume — which is why volume analysis is about relative changes, not absolute values.

Reading Volume Bars

Most charting platforms show a volume bar chart below the price chart. Each bar represents the total volume in that candle's time period. The bar is usually coloured green (if that candle's close > open) or red (if close < open). The height of the bar relative to surrounding bars is what you read.

The Average Volume Baseline

To judge whether a volume bar is "high" or "low," you need a baseline. Most traders use a 20-period moving average of volume (shown as a line on the volume pane). If the current bar is above this line, volume is above average. Below it = below average.

Volume LevelMeaningAction

1.5–2× averageAbove average — strong participationGood confirmation for entry
0.8–1.5× averageNormal rangeNeutral — look for other confluences
<0.8× averageBelow average — low participation, potential noiseCaution — avoid entering on low volume moves

Volume and Price — Four Scenarios

The relationship between price direction and volume creates four distinct scenarios, each with a specific market interpretation:

Price UP + Volume UP
BULLISH STRENGTH
The most bullish scenario. Buyers are actively participating at increasing levels of commitment. Institutions are buying. Trend is healthy and likely to continue. This is the market's way of saying "we all agree — up."
Price DOWN + Volume UP
BEARISH STRENGTH
The most bearish scenario. Sellers are dumping aggressively. Panic or large institutional liquidation is driving the fall. This kind of selling can persist for sessions or even weeks. Don't buy into this — wait for volume to dry up first.
Price UP + Volume DOWN
WEAK RALLY — WARNING
Price is rising but fewer people are participating. This is a "thin air" rally — easy to reverse. Often seen at the late stage of a bull run or during low-liquidity afternoon sessions. Be cautious about chasing this move — it lacks institutional backing.
Price DOWN + Volume DOWN
WEAK DECLINE — NEUTRAL
Price is falling but nobody is urgently selling. This is a "lack of buyers" decline rather than active selling. Often seen in healthy pullbacks within an uptrend. This is the scenario to look for before buying a dip — selling is not accelerating.
Nifty Application — June 2024 Post-Election Rally
After the sharp June 4 election-day fall (Bear Marubozu, high volume), Nifty began recovering. June 5–7 showed: Price UP + Volume declining gradually — a weak rally scenario. This warned that the recovery might not be sustainable. Indeed, Nifty stalled near 22,700 before resuming the decline. Traders who watched volume alongside price avoided buying the bounce too early. The real recovery came only when volume expanded again above 22,000 in mid-June.

Volume Breakouts — Real vs Fake

Not all breakouts above resistance or below support are real. Many are "false breakouts" — price briefly crosses the level and then snaps back. Volume is the primary tool to distinguish real breakouts from fakes.

✓
Real breakout: volume ≥ 1.5× the 20-day average

A genuine breakout attracts new buyers/sellers who weren't in the previous range. This creates a volume expansion. The breakout candle should be wide-range (large body) and the volume bar should be clearly above average.

✗
Fake breakout: volume below average on the break

Price crosses the level on below-average volume = no conviction. The "breakout" is just noise — a few stop-loss orders triggered or thin liquidity. Price typically reverses back into the range within 1–3 candles.

?
Marginal breakout: wait for the close

If volume is in the 1.0–1.5× range during the breakout candle, wait for the candle to close above the level before entering. A close above the level with even moderate volume is more reliable than a wick poke on high volume.

Nifty Breakout Comparison — 22,500 Level · 2024
Attempt 1 (Feb 12): Nifty crossed 22,500 briefly, closed at 22,491. Volume was 0.7× average. Price returned below 22,300 within 3 sessions — fake breakout.

Attempt 2 (Feb 20): Nifty crossed 22,500 and closed at 22,570. Volume was 2.1× average — the largest volume session in 3 weeks. This was a genuine breakout. Nifty continued to 22,526 high over the next 2 weeks. The only difference between the two attempts was volume.

Volume Climax and Exhaustion

A volume climax is an extreme, abnormal spike in volume that often marks the end of a directional move rather than a continuation. When everyone who wants to sell has sold (selling climax) or everyone who wants to buy has bought (buying climax), there are no more participants left to push price further — and it reverses.

Selling Climax (Bullish Reversal Signal)

Characteristics: very large volume spike on a down candle, often with a long lower wick. The high volume represents the last desperate sellers unloading positions. After the selling climax, price often rebounds sharply because all the sellers have exited.

Nifty Selling Climax — March 2020 COVID Low
Nifty's COVID low was set on March 23, 2020, at 7,511. The volume on that day was 5–6× the 3-month average — an extreme climax. That single session represented the maximum panic — and was the absolute bottom. Over the next 18 months, Nifty rallied from 7,511 to 18,600. The selling climax did not mean "buy immediately" — but it was a clear signal that the panic selling was reaching exhaustion.

Buying Climax (Bearish Reversal Signal)

Characteristics: extreme volume spike on an up candle, often at a new high. The high volume represents a feeding frenzy of late buyers piling in at the top — who will soon become trapped as price reverses. When you see an extreme volume spike at or near resistance/ATH, it is often the last burst of buying before a correction.

⚠️

Climax caution: Volume climaxes on news events (RBI policy, budget day, election results) are different from organic climaxes. News-driven extreme volumes don't always mean reversal — the news itself may have permanently changed the fundamental picture. Always read the context alongside the volume data.

Opening (9:15–9:45) — High volume, volatile

Market digests overnight news, global cues, pre-open auction results. Volume spikes are normal. Do not use opening volume spikes to validate breakouts — they're expected. Wait for the 9:30–10:00 window to normalise before reading volume.

Mid
Midday (11:00–1:30) — Low volume, slow moves

This is the quietest period. Breakouts on low midday volume are often false. If price breaks a level during lunch hours, require a close confirmation and don't over-trade.

3PM
Closing (2:45–3:30) — Volume surges, often directional

FII and institutional order execution happens in the last 45 minutes. Volume expansion in this window is highly meaningful. A directional move on closing volume often sets the next day's opening bias.

7

OBV — On Balance Volume

On Balance Volume (OBV) is a simple but powerful indicator that tracks cumulative volume directionally. When price closes up, the period's volume is added. When price closes down, volume is subtracted. The result is a running total that shows whether volume is flowing into or out of an asset.

OBV is most useful for divergence analysis — when price and OBV move in opposite directions, it often precedes a price reversal (covered in Section 9).

💡

OBV practical use: In an uptrend, OBV should also be making higher highs. If Nifty makes a new high but OBV fails to make a new high (bearish divergence), selling pressure is building beneath the surface — a warning sign for the trend. Conversely, OBV making new highs while price hasn't yet = accumulation (bullish lead indicator).

OBV Setup on Charting Platforms

Add OBV as a separate indicator panel below your price chart. Use it alongside price for confirmation: new price high + new OBV high = healthy trend. New price high + OBV flat or declining = distribution phase, potential top forming. Most Indian charting platforms (Zerodha Kite, TradingView) include OBV under "indicators."

Volume in the F&O Context

For Nifty options and futures traders, volume analysis extends beyond spot chart volume to open interest (OI) changes — which are the futures/options equivalent of volume analysis.

OI ChangePrice ChangeInterpretation
OI risingPrice risingNew money entering longs — bullish trend strength
OI risingPrice fallingNew money entering shorts — bearish trend strength
OI fallingPrice risingShort covering — bullish but less strong (temporary)
OI fallingPrice fallingLong unwinding — bearish but less strong (may stabilise)

In TradeZen's Options Analysis tool, the OI chart for each strike shows this relationship in real time. When Nifty is rallying and OI at the ATM call is rising rapidly, it signals that fresh longs are building — bullish. When OI at the ATM put is declining while Nifty falls, it signals put writers covering — the fall may slow.

💡

PCR (Put-Call Ratio): The ratio of put OI to call OI. PCR > 1.2 = excessive bearish bets = contrarian bullish signal (market often reverses up). PCR < 0.7 = excessive bullish bets = contrarian bearish signal. Extreme PCR readings with volume spikes = potential reversal zone.

9

Volume Divergence

Volume divergence is when price and volume move in opposite directions — a powerful early warning signal. There are two types:

⚠
Bearish divergence: price makes higher high, volume makes lower high

Price is going up on declining participation — the rally is running out of fuel. Institutional sellers are absorbing the buying. This is a warning to tighten stops on long positions and avoid adding new longs near resistance.

✓
Bullish divergence: price makes lower low, volume makes lower low too

Price is declining but on decreasing sell pressure — the decline is running out of steam. Sellers are exhausted. Combined with a support level and a reversal pattern (Hammer, Engulfing), this is a high-probability long setup.

Nifty Volume Divergence — Sept–Oct 2024
Nifty made a new ATH at 25,670 on Sept 26, 2024. The volume on the high day was notably below the volume from the prior ATH at 25,235 three weeks earlier. Bearish volume divergence at ATH — the new high had less conviction than the prior high. Combined with the near-Gravestone Doji at the top, this signalled distribution. Nifty corrected to 23,893 over the next 3 weeks.

Common Mistakes & Key Takeaways

🙈
Trading Without Volume Panel
Many beginners trade on charts without a volume indicator. Removing a crucial confirmation tool and trading on price alone massively reduces edge.
Fix: Always have a volume panel on your chart. Add a 20-period volume MA to it. Never take a breakout trade without checking the volume bar.
📰
Misreading News Volume
Seeing a high-volume sell day (Budget day, RBI policy) and treating it as a selling climax signal to buy. News events can sustain high volume for days without the reversal you expect.
Fix: Separate "organic" volume climaxes from news-driven volume. News events require fundamental context before treating volume as a reversal signal.
🕐
Judging Opening Volume
Seeing high volume at 9:15 AM and concluding a breakout is "confirmed." Opening volume is structurally high every day — it is not a reliable breakout filter.
Fix: Use the 10:00 AM volume as your first reliable intraday volume benchmark. Compare candles from 10 AM onward against their recent session averages.
📊
Ignoring OI in F&O Trades
Trading Nifty options based only on spot price chart without checking OI changes. Missing the signal that a rally is only short covering (temporary) vs. fresh buying (sustained).
Fix: For any Nifty options position, check OI vs price relationship. Use TradeZen's Options Analysis OI chart alongside the spot chart.
📉
Only Watching Volume Spikes
Only reacting when volume is very high and missing the signal of gradually declining volume during a move — which is equally important (warns of trend exhaustion).
Fix: Watch volume trends, not just individual bars. Is volume expanding or contracting over the last 5–10 candles? A trend of declining volume is as important as a single spike.
🔄
Using Volume Alone
Buying every high-volume green candle without checking if price is at a key level, in the right trend, and showing a pattern. Volume confirms — it doesn't initiate trades.
Fix: Volume is a confirmation tool, not a trigger. The trigger comes from S/R levels and candlestick patterns. Volume confirms the trigger's validity.
⚡ Key Takeaways — Chapter 4
✓Volume is the market's lie detector — it confirms whether price moves have institutional backing or are just retail noise
✓The four volume-price combinations: Price↑Vol↑ = bullish strength; Price↓Vol↑ = bearish strength; Price↑Vol↓ = weak rally; Price↓Vol↓ = weak decline
✓Breakouts require volume ≥ 1.5× average to be reliable — below-average volume breakouts fail more than 60% of the time
✓Volume climaxes (extreme spikes) often mark turning points — especially when they appear with reversal candlestick patterns
✓Nifty volume follows a U-shape intraday: high at open, low midday, high at close. Adjust your interpretation accordingly
✓OBV divergence — when OBV doesn't confirm price new highs/lows — is an early warning of trend weakness or reversal
✓In F&O, OI changes are the volume equivalent — rising OI with rising price = fresh longs (bullish); rising OI with falling price = fresh shorts (bearish)
✓Volume is a confirmation tool, not a trigger — use it to validate trades identified by S/R levels and candlestick patterns
