CPR — Central Pivot Range

The CPR is the single most powerful intraday tool in Nifty trading. Calculated from the prior day's OHLC, it gives you three precise levels — TC, PP, BC — that define the day's bias before the market even opens. Used correctly, it transforms reactive trading into proactive trading.

What is CPR?

The Central Pivot Range (CPR) is a three-level price structure — Top Pivot (TC), Pivot Point (PP), and Bottom Pivot (BC) — calculated using the previous trading day's High, Low, and Close. It represents the fair value zone and directional bias for the next session.

CPR was popularised in Indian markets by Camarilla traders and later by system traders using it as the primary intraday bias tool. What makes CPR special compared to most indicators is that it is calculated before the market opens — giving you precise, objective levels to plan your trades before the opening bell.

🎯

Why CPR works: Institutional players, option writers, and professional traders calculate CPR levels daily. When enough participants use the same levels, price reacts to them — creating self-fulfilling behaviour at TC, PP, and BC. This is not a theoretical concept — you will see price stop at these levels repeatedly in Nifty's intraday action.

2

The Formula — How CPR is Calculated

CPR uses the prior day's High (H), Low (L), and Close (C). The three levels are:

PP
Pivot Point = (H + L + C) ÷ 3

The central anchor of the CPR. The arithmetic mean of the prior day's three key prices. If today's price stays above PP, the bias is bullish; below PP = bearish.

TC
Top Pivot (TC) = (H + L) ÷ 2

The upper boundary of the CPR zone. Price above TC = strong bullish bias. TC acts as resistance when price approaches from below, and as support once broken above.

BC
Bottom Pivot (BC) = PP − (TC − BC) = (TC + BC) ÷ 2 resolve → BC = PP × 2 − TC

The lower boundary. Price below BC = strong bearish bias. BC acts as support from above and resistance once broken below.

Worked Calculation — Nifty Jan 15, 2024
Prior day (Jan 14) OHLC: O=21,650, H=21,889, L=21,612, C=21,850

PP = (21,889 + 21,612 + 21,850) ÷ 3 = 65,351 ÷ 3 = 21,783.67
TC = (21,889 + 21,612) ÷ 2 = 43,501 ÷ 2 = 21,750.50
BC = PP × 2 − TC = 21,783.67 × 2 − 21,750.50 = 21,816.84

Wait — TC < PP which means TC and BC labels are swapped. When TC < PP < BC, rename: TC = 21,816.84 (higher), BC = 21,750.50 (lower). This is normal — the label "TC" always refers to the higher value, "BC" to the lower. CPR zone for Jan 15: 21,751 – 21,817.

Reading the CPR Width

The width of the CPR zone (TC − BC) is one of the most important things to check each morning. It tells you what kind of day to expect before the session begins.

Width (Nifty)InterpretationTrading Approach
< 50 pts (Narrow)Tight range day expected — market indecision, equal prior day forcesRange-bound strategies; look for breakout from CPR zone for momentum trades
50–100 pts (Normal)Average day expectedStandard CPR levels — respect TC as resistance, BC as support until broken
>150 pts (Wide)High volatility day expected — strong directional prior dayPrice unlikely to chop inside CPR — expect strong trends; look for reversals at TC/BC extremes
Nifty CPR Width Context — Election Week June 2024
June 4, 2024 (election result day): Nifty H=22,527, L=21,964, C=21,884. This created an extremely wide CPR for June 5: TC ≈ 22,246, BC ≈ 21,891 — width of 355 points. This told traders immediately: June 5 would be a directional day with high volatility. The wide CPR meant price would likely trend strongly in one direction rather than chop. Nifty trended upward through the CPR zone on June 5, recovering to 22,621.
4

CPR as a Magnet — Virgin CPR

A Virgin CPR is a CPR zone that price has never traded through during its calculation day. When the next session's price approaches an untested CPR from a previous day, that CPR acts like a magnet — price is drawn toward it and then either breaks through or reverses.

This concept is powerful because the CPR represents an "unfinished" zone — the market never fully explored that price area. Institutional players know this and often use it as a target.

Buy
Price below a virgin CPR from prior day → bullish magnet

If yesterday's market opened above CPR and never revisited it, today price will often rally toward the prior day's CPR from below. Look for confirmation candles before the CPR as entry zones.

Sell
Price above a virgin CPR from prior day → bearish magnet

If yesterday's market opened below CPR and never retested it, today price will often fall back toward the prior day's CPR from above. Use this as a target for short trades.

💡

Multi-day virgin CPR stacking: If a CPR has been untested for 2–3 consecutive days, its magnetic pull becomes very strong. The longer an area remains untested, the more likely price will eventually visit it. These zones are excellent targets for swing trades.

5

CPR Scenarios — Bull, Bear, Range

The opening position of price relative to CPR at 9:15 AM sets the day's primary bias. There are three core scenarios:

Bull Day Setup
Open above TC
The day opens above the entire CPR zone. Bias is immediately bullish. TC becomes the first support. Buy dips to TC with targets at Camarilla R1/R2 above. Avoid short trades until TC is broken with a close.
Bear Day Setup
Open below BC
The day opens below the entire CPR zone. Bias is immediately bearish. BC becomes the first resistance. Short rallies to BC with targets at Camarilla S1/S2 below. Avoid long trades until BC is broken upward.
Range Day Setup
Open inside CPR (between TC and BC)
The day opens within the CPR zone. Expect chop and two-sided price action. This is the most challenging scenario — price will test both TC and BC before committing to direction. Strategy: wait for a clean break and close above TC (go long) or below BC (go short) before entering directional trades. Inside-CPR is not an entry — it is a waiting zone.
Nifty Bull Day Setup — Feb 9, 2024
CPR for Feb 9: TC = 21,945, PP = 21,910, BC = 21,875.
Nifty opened at 21,988 — above TC by 43 points. Immediate bullish bias established. First pullback came at 9:45 to 21,953 — touching TC from above. That was the long entry: entry at 21,953, stop below TC at 21,920. Nifty rallied through the session to 22,097. Risk: 33 pts, Reward: 144 pts — a 4.4:1 trade from a pre-calculated CPR level.

CPR Breakout Trades

When price is inside the CPR at the open and then breaks out, it often makes a strong directional move. The CPR provides the reference for the breakout and the stop.

1
Wait for price to enter the CPR zone

If price opens outside the CPR and then enters it (retest), or if price opens inside CPR — in both cases, this sets up a potential breakout.

2
Wait for a 15-min candle to close outside the CPR

A candle that closes above TC = bullish breakout signal. A close below BC = bearish breakout. Do not enter on wick pokes through the level — wait for a full candle close.

3
Entry on breakout candle close; stop inside CPR

Enter at the close of the breakout candle. Stop goes to the middle of the CPR (PP) or the opposite boundary (TC for long breakout, BC for short). This gives a tight, logical stop.

4
Target: Camarilla R1/R2 or prior day high/low

The first target for a CPR bullish breakout is Camarilla R1 (above TC). Second target is R2 or prior day high. Use 1:2 risk:reward minimum. TradeZen Trade Flow shows all these levels automatically.

CPR Reversal Trades

When price comes from outside the CPR and approaches TC or BC, these levels often act as reversal points — especially when combined with candlestick patterns and volume confirmation.

Setup 1
Rally to TC from below — short setup

Price opens below CPR (bear day), rallies up to test TC from below. TC acts as resistance. A Shooting Star, Bearish Engulfing, or Doji at TC = short entry. Stop above TC + 20 pts. Target: BC and below.

Setup 2
Fall to BC from above — long setup

Price opens above CPR (bull day), falls to test BC from above. BC acts as support. A Hammer, Bullish Engulfing, or Doji at BC = long entry. Stop below BC − 20 pts. Target: TC and above.

Nifty CPR Reversal — BC Support Trade · Mar 15, 2024
CPR for Mar 15: TC=22,125, PP=22,075, BC=22,025. Nifty opened at 22,158 (above TC — bull day). By 10:30 AM, Nifty had pulled back and touched 22,030 — exactly at the BC level. A Hammer candle formed on the 15-min chart at BC. Long entry: 22,048 (close of Hammer), stop: 22,005 (below BC), target: 22,200+ (above TC). Nifty recovered to 22,193 by 1:30 PM. Risk 43 pts, reward 145 pts.

CPR + Camarilla Levels

CPR is most powerful when combined with Camarilla pivot levels (H3/H4/L3/L4). These are calculated from the same prior-day OHLC and give the day's extended support and resistance zones beyond the CPR.

Common Mistakes & Key Takeaways

📅
Not Checking CPR Before the Open
Opening TradeZen at 9:20 AM after the market has moved. Missing the opening position vs CPR means missing the day's bias entirely.
Fix: Open Trade Flow before 9:15 AM every day. The 5 minutes of CPR review saves hours of reactive decision-making.
🔀
Trading Inside CPR
Entering directional trades when price is still inside the CPR zone. This leads to getting stopped out repeatedly as price churns between TC and BC.
Fix: When price is inside CPR, wait for a 15-min close outside (above TC or below BC) before entering any directional trade.
📏
Ignoring CPR Width
Using the same trading strategy regardless of CPR width. Applying range-bound strategies on a wide-CPR trending day leads to repeated losses from being stopped out.
Fix: Check CPR width first. Narrow (<50 pts): range strategies. Wide (>150 pts): trend following strategies. Adjust your approach each morning.
🎯
Using CPR Alone Without Confluence
Entering every TC/BC touch as a trade without looking for candlestick confirmation or volume support. CPR levels that align with S/R, EMA, or volume spikes are far more powerful.
Fix: Require at least one additional confluent factor at any CPR level before entering. CPR + Hammer + above-average volume = high-probability trade.
↩️
Forgetting the Virgin CPR Concept
Treating each day in isolation and not maintaining a record of prior days' untested CPR zones. Missing powerful magnet-level opportunities across multiple sessions.
Fix: Mark the prior 2–3 days' CPR zones on your daily chart. Flag any that were never tested. These become priority levels for the next session.
⏰
Using CPR on Daily Chart (Wrong Timeframe)
CPR calculated from daily OHLC is for intraday use on 5-min/15-min charts. Using it on a daily chart doesn't make sense — it's already contained within that day's candle.
Fix: Daily CPR → use on 5-min or 15-min charts. For positional trading, calculate weekly or monthly CPR using weekly/monthly OHLC.
