What is CPR?

Central Pivot Range (CPR) is a set of three price levels calculated from the previous day's High, Low, and Close. Unlike a single pivot point, CPR gives you a zone — a range of price where the market tends to react.

The three levels are TC (Top Central), PP (Pivot Point), and BC (Bottom Central). When price opens above TC, the market bias is bullish. When it opens below BC, bias is bearish.

How Levels Are Calculated

All levels come from the previous trading day's High (H), Low (L), and Close (C). They are fixed for the entire trading day and never change intraday.

1
Pivot Point (PP)

PP = (H + L + C) / 3
The central anchor. Price above PP = bullish, below PP = bearish.

2
Bottom Central (BC) and Top Central (TC)

BC = (H + L) / 2    TC = (2 × PP) − BC
BC and TC form the CPR zone. TC is always the higher level, BC the lower.

3
Support & Resistance (R1–R3, S1–S3)

R1 = 2×PP − L   R2 = PP + (H−L)   R3 = H + 2×(PP−L)
S1 = 2×PP − H   S2 = PP − (H−L)   S3 = L − 2×(H−PP)

💡

The TradeZen engine fetches previous day OHLC from yfinance at startup and computes all levels automatically. You never need to enter them manually.

Virgin CPR

A Virgin CPR occurs when the previous session's price action never touched or traded through the CPR zone (between BC and TC). It is one of the highest-probability setups in CPR trading.

🔮

Why it matters: When price has never tested a CPR zone, it acts like a magnet. The first touch almost always triggers a strong reaction — either a powerful reversal or an explosive breakout.

→
Reversal on first touch

Price reaches TC from below, reversal candle forms — sell signal. Or price reaches BC from above, bounce candle forms — buy signal.

→
Breakout through virgin zone

Price blasts through the untouched CPR with a strong body candle — this is an explosive momentum trade. Ride in the breakout direction.

⚠️

The purple glowing badge and banner on the CPR Monitor page means today's CPR is virgin. Once price enters the zone, the badge disappears and a 🔮 VIRGIN CPR alert fires on the chart.

Camarilla Levels

Camarilla levels are eight intraday support/resistance levels derived from the previous day's range. They complement CPR by giving tighter, more precise entry points.

LevelFormulaTrading Use
H4 C + (H−L) × 1.1 / 2 Breakout long — once price breaks H4 with volume, strong bull momentum. Ride aggressively.
H3 C + (H−L) × 1.1 / 4 Mean reversion sell — price often reverses sharply at H3. Short here, SL above H4.
L3 C − (H−L) × 1.1 / 4 Mean reversion buy — price often bounces at L3. Long here, SL below L4.
L4 C − (H−L) × 1.1 / 2 Breakout short — once price breaks L4 with volume, strong bear momentum. Ride aggressively.
📌

H1, H2, L1, L2 are intermediate levels shown on the price ladder. They act as minor support/resistance but the monitor only fires alerts at H3, H4, L3, L4 where the significant reactions occur.

Three Strategies

↕️
Mean Reversion
Works on wide CPR days when price is oscillating inside the zone. Buy near BC, sell near TC. Exit at PP.
Setup: Price inside CPR
Buy: Near BC (+5 pts)
Sell: Near TC (−5 pts)
SL: 10–15 pts beyond level
Target: PP or opposite level
🚀
CPR Breakout
Works on narrow CPR days. Enter on confirmed breakout above TC or below BC. Best combined with ORB breakout direction.
Setup: Candle closes outside CPR
Entry: On close of breakout candle
SL: TC − 15 (bull) / BC + 15 (bear)
T1: R1 (bull) / S1 (bear)
T2: R2 (bull) / S2 (bear)
📏
Camarilla
Works throughout the day regardless of CPR width. L3 = buy zone, H3 = sell zone. H4/L4 = trend breakout entry.
Buy: Near L3, SL below L4
Sell: Near H3, SL above H4
Breakout Long: Above H4 with volume
Breakout Short: Below L4 with volume
Target: H3/L3 for breakout trades

Noise Filtering Rules

Not every alert is a trade. The monitor applies these rules to filter out low-quality signals:

✅

Candle body ≥ 45% of range for breakouts. Doji candles and candles with long wicks are ignored for breakout classification — they show indecision, not momentum.

✅

Retest must happen ≥ 2 candles after breakout. An immediate pullback is not a retest — it's just noise. The monitor waits for at least 2 more candles before firing a retest alert.

✅

Reversal requires price to have moved ≥ 1 CPR width away. If price was only barely above TC, crossing back inside is not a reversal — it's just the CPR zone being tested. A true reversal needs price to have been extended.

✅

Each candle is processed exactly once. No duplicate alerts for the same time candle, even if the page refreshes.

⚠️

Camarilla alerts fire only when price wicks to the level and closes back. A candle that just grazes the level with its low and then closes away — that's the signal. Not a candle that closes through the level (that's a different setup).

Reading the Chart

The CPR Monitor chart shows 5-minute Nifty candles with all levels and alert markers overlaid.

TC line (green solid)

Top of CPR zone. Price closing above = bullish confirmation.

PP line (orange solid)

Pivot point. Mid-zone reference. Acts as short-term S/R inside CPR.

BC line (red solid)

Bottom of CPR zone. Price closing below = bearish confirmation.

H3 / H4 lines (blue dashed)

Camarilla resistance levels. H3 = mean reversion sell zone. H4 = breakout level.

L3 / L4 lines (purple dashed)

Camarilla support levels. L3 = mean reversion buy zone. L4 = breakdown level.

▲
Upward arrow markers (below candle)

Buy signal alerts — Virgin CPR touch, bullish breakout, breakout failure buy, L3 bounce, etc.

▼
Downward arrow markers (above candle)

Sell signal alerts — bearish breakout, H3 rejection, breakout failure sell, CPR reversal, etc.

🎯

Tip: The most reliable setup is: Narrow CPR + price opens above TC + ORB forms above TC + CPR Retest alert fires. This is a textbook continuation long with all three systems aligned.
