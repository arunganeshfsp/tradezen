VWAP in Practice

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the intraday benchmark that every institutional trader watches. It is the price at which the most volume has transacted during the session — making it the single most important intraday support and resistance level in Nifty trading.

What is VWAP?

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the average price at which a security has traded throughout the day, weighted by volume. A trade of 1000 lots at 22,500 counts more toward VWAP than 10 lots at 22,600. This weighting by volume is what makes VWAP the "true" average price — not just a simple arithmetic mean.

VWAP resets to zero at the start of each trading session (9:15 AM IST) and updates continuously throughout the day. Because of this reset, VWAP is a pure intraday indicator — it does not carry over to the next session. This is different from a moving average, which uses historical data.

📌

VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) ÷ Σ(Volume) — summed from 9:15 AM to the current time. Early in the day (9:15–10:00), VWAP is heavily influenced by opening range volume. By afternoon, it stabilises and large single trades have less impact on its value.

2

How VWAP is Calculated

For each period (candle), calculate the Typical Price = (High + Low + Close) ÷ 3. Then multiply by that period's volume. VWAP at any point = sum of all (Typical Price × Volume) from session start ÷ sum of all volumes from session start.

Simplified VWAP Calculation — First 3 Candles of Session
9:15 candle: H=22,100, L=21,950, C=22,050 → TP = 22,033. Volume = 50,000 lots. Contribution = 22,033 × 50,000 = 1,101,650,000
9:30 candle: H=22,080, L=22,010, C=22,060 → TP = 22,050. Volume = 30,000. Contribution = 661,500,000
9:45 candle: H=22,120, L=22,040, C=22,100 → TP = 22,087. Volume = 20,000. Contribution = 441,733,000

VWAP at 9:45 = (1,101,650,000 + 661,500,000 + 441,733,000) ÷ (50,000 + 30,000 + 20,000) = 2,204,883,000 ÷ 100,000 = 22,049

Notice VWAP is closest to the high-volume 9:15 candle (22,033) — that's the volume weighting at work.
⚠️

You don't need to calculate VWAP manually. All major Indian charting platforms (Zerodha Kite, TradingView, Upstox Pro) show VWAP as a built-in indicator on intraday charts. Add it to your 5-min or 15-min Nifty chart and it calculates automatically in real time.

3

VWAP as Intraday Bias

The simplest and most powerful VWAP rule: price above VWAP = bullish intraday bias; price below VWAP = bearish intraday bias. This is the institutional standard — most large funds execute buy orders when price is below VWAP (to get a better-than-average price) and sell when above VWAP (to get a better-than-average sale price).

VWAP ZONES — INTRADAY BIAS GUIDE
Price well above VWAP (+1 std dev or more) STRONG BULL — hold longs, avoid new longs here
Price above VWAP (within +1 std dev) BULLISH — look for long entries on VWAP pulls
Price at VWAP (± a few points) NEUTRAL — breakout zone, both directions possible
Price below VWAP (within -1 std dev) BEARISH — look for short entries on VWAP pops
Price well below VWAP (-1 std dev or more) STRONG BEAR — hold shorts, avoid new shorts here
🎯

The VWAP bias rule for Nifty: If Nifty opens above VWAP at 9:15 AM AND the CPR analysis confirms a bull bias (open above TC), you have double confirmation. In this scenario, buy any pullback to VWAP with high confidence. The reverse applies for bearish setups.

4

VWAP Standard Deviation Bands

Most VWAP indicators include standard deviation bands (±1 SD, ±2 SD) plotted above and below the VWAP line. These bands show how far price has deviated from the volume-weighted average and create natural support/resistance zones.

BandLocationMeaningTrading Use
+2 SDFar above VWAPSignificantly overbought intradayProfit target for bull trades; potential short fade area
+1 SDAbove VWAPMild overboughtFirst profit target for long entries at VWAP
VWAP (0)CentreFair value for the sessionDynamic support (bull day) or resistance (bear day)
-1 SDBelow VWAPMild oversoldFirst profit target for short entries at VWAP; potential long bounce zone
-2 SDFar below VWAPSignificantly oversold intradayProfit target for shorts; potential mean-reversion long setup
💡

Nifty VWAP band statistics: On a typical day, Nifty price stays within ±1 SD of VWAP approximately 68% of the time (standard deviation property). On high-volatility days (budget, election results, RBI policy), the +2 SD or -2 SD bands may be reached. These extreme bands are where mean-reversion trades offer the best risk/reward.

5

VWAP Pullback Trades

The most common and reliable VWAP-based trade: buy a pullback to VWAP on a bull day; short a rally to VWAP on a bear day. The logic is simple — institutional buyers want to buy below VWAP to get a better-than-average price. When price returns to VWAP, they add to positions, creating the bounce you can ride.

Bull Day VWAP Pullback — Setup Rules

1
Confirm it's a bull day first

Price opened above VWAP (or quickly moved above it after open). CPR analysis shows open above TC or price has sustained above VWAP for at least 30–45 minutes.

2
Wait for a pullback to VWAP

Price retreats from the session high toward VWAP. This is not a breakdown — it is a healthy pullback in an uptrend. The pullback should happen on declining volume (not panic selling).

3
Enter on a reversal candle AT VWAP

A Hammer, Doji, or bullish candle forming right at VWAP = entry signal. Enter at the candle's close. Stop: 20–30 points below VWAP (below -1 SD if bands are shown).

4
Target: +1 SD band or prior session high

First profit target is the +1 SD VWAP band. Second target is the prior high of the session. Exit 50–75% at first target, run the rest with a trailing stop at VWAP.

Nifty VWAP Pullback Trade · Jan 22, 2024
Session opened bullish — Nifty at 21,988, VWAP at 21,950. By 10:30, Nifty ran to 22,097. By 11:15, it pulled back to 21,963 — almost exactly at VWAP (21,958). A bullish Hammer formed on the 15-min chart at this level. Entry: 21,970 (Hammer close), Stop: 21,930 (below VWAP -1 SD at 21,925), Target: 22,097 (prior high). Nifty recovered and made a new session high at 22,135. Risk 40 pts, Reward 165 pts.
6

VWAP Reclaim Trade

A VWAP reclaim happens when price breaks below VWAP (going bearish), then fights back and closes above it again. This transition from bearish to bullish is a powerful signal — it means buyers overwhelmed sellers and reclaimed control of the session's average price.

1
Price drops below VWAP — bearish phase begins

The initial break below VWAP is a negative signal. Most intraday traders turn bearish at this point. Short sellers pile in expecting further decline.

2
Price churns near VWAP, refusing to follow through lower

If price breaks below VWAP but doesn't continue lower — keeps coming back to VWAP — this is a warning sign that short sellers are trapped. Low volume on the decline below VWAP strengthens this read.

3
A 15-min candle closes ABOVE VWAP = reclaim confirmed

This is the reclaim entry. Shorts who entered on the VWAP break are now trapped below and will cover as price rises, adding fuel to the move. Enter long at the close of the reclaim candle.

The VWAP reclaim trade works best during the Prime Zone (10:15 AM – 1:00 PM) when there is still significant session time remaining. A reclaim near 3:00 PM has less follow-through potential.

7

VWAP + CPR Confluence

When VWAP aligns with a CPR level (TC, PP, or BC), the resulting support/resistance zone is significantly stronger than either level alone. This happens because two entirely independent calculation methods point to the same price — doubling the participant base that is watching that level.

VWAP + CPR Confluence — Nifty Feb 19, 2024
CPR BC for Feb 19 was at 21,875. By 10:45 AM, VWAP had settled at 21,882 — just 7 points above CPR BC. VWAP (21,882) and CPR BC (21,875) had converged into a single support zone.

When Nifty pulled back to this zone at 11:00 AM and touched 21,878, the bounce was immediate and sharp — Nifty ran 150 points to 22,028 within two hours. The confluence of two major levels created the strongest possible support zone in that session.

How Often Do VWAP and CPR Align?

VWAP is dynamic (changes throughout the day). CPR is fixed (calculated pre-market). They will naturally converge at various points during the session. The most significant convergence happens when VWAP approaches a CPR level during the Prime Zone (10:15 AM – 1:00 PM) — this is when the alignment carries the most weight for intraday entries.

8

Anchored VWAP

Anchored VWAP (AVWAP) is a VWAP calculated from a specific historical starting point — such as a major swing low, a major news event, or the beginning of a new bull/bear phase. It gives the average price since that anchor point, making it a multi-day or multi-week reference level.

Anchor PointPurposeUse Case
IPO dateAverage price since listingIdentify if stock is above/below IPO buyers' average cost
Major low (swing/correction)Average price since recovery beganSupport level during pullbacks in bull trend
Earnings announcementAverage price since major newsFair value established since earnings — support/resistance
52-week high/lowAverage since extreme level hitReference for whether trend is above/below "ATH buyers' average"
💡

Practical AVWAP for Nifty: Anchor VWAP to Nifty's major correction low (e.g., Oct 2023 low at 18,837). If current Nifty is above the AVWAP from that low, the long-term buyers from the recovery are in profit and bullish sentiment is intact. If Nifty falls below this AVWAP, it signals distribution of those positions — a medium-term bearish signal.

9

Institutional Use of VWAP

Understanding why institutions use VWAP makes you a better trader — because you can predict where institutional order flow will appear. Large institutions (mutual funds, insurance companies, FIIs) use VWAP as their execution benchmark.

Buy
Institutional buying: price below VWAP

When a mutual fund needs to buy 1000 crore worth of Nifty, they use algorithmic VWAP orders to execute over the day at or below VWAP. Each time price dips below VWAP, their algorithm places buy orders — which creates the "VWAP support" effect you see on your chart.

Sell
Institutional selling: price above VWAP

Similarly, when selling large positions, institutions sell above VWAP to get a better-than-average execution price. Their sell orders above VWAP create resistance — which is why VWAP becomes resistance on bear days.

This institutional algorithm behaviour is self-reinforcing. Because institutions use VWAP-based execution, the levels create real order flow at VWAP — which makes VWAP "work" as a technical level. It is one of the clearest examples of technical analysis creating its own reality through participant behaviour.

Common Mistakes & Key Takeaways

📅
Using VWAP on Daily Chart
VWAP resets every session. On a daily chart, the "VWAP" shown is not the same metric — it becomes a multi-day calculation that loses its intraday significance.
Fix: Only use VWAP on intraday charts (5-min, 15-min). On daily charts, use EMAs instead.
⏰
Trading VWAP Crosses in First 15 Min
VWAP is unstable in the first 15 minutes due to the opening auction's large volume skewing the average. Early VWAP crosses are unreliable — too much noise.
Fix: Ignore VWAP signals before 9:30 AM. Wait until at least 9:45 AM before using VWAP levels for entries.
↕️
Treating VWAP as an Exact Level
Expecting price to bounce off VWAP at exactly that price point. VWAP has a zone around it (±10–15 pts for Nifty) within which it acts as support/resistance.
Fix: Enter when price is within 15 pts of VWAP and shows a reversal candle. Don't wait for an exact tick hit before placing the order.
🔄
Ignoring Late-Day VWAP Reclaims
A VWAP reclaim after 2:30 PM has very little time for follow-through. Entering full position size near close based on VWAP reclaim is risky — the move may not have time to develop.
Fix: VWAP reclaims work best between 10:00–1:30 PM. After 2:30 PM, only take reclaims with very tight stops and reduced size.
📊
Using VWAP Alone for Entry
Buying every VWAP touch without a confirming candle pattern or volume check. Not every VWAP test holds — some break through and continue lower.
Fix: Require a reversal candle (Hammer, Engulfing) AND volume confirmation at the VWAP level before entering. Naked VWAP touches have lower success rates.
🔀
Forgetting VWAP + CPR Confluence
Tracking VWAP and CPR separately without noticing when they align. Missing the strongest S/R zones of the day.
Fix: When VWAP gets within 20 pts of a CPR level (TC, PP, or BC), mark that zone as a priority level for that time period. These confluences create the day's most reliable entry points.
