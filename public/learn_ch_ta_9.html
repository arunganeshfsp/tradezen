Options Chain Basics

The chain tells you the battleground: The strike with maximum call OI = resistance (call writers defending it). The strike with maximum put OI = support (put writers defending it). The expiry price tends to settle between these two extremes — this is the Max Pain concept.

2

Open Interest — The Big Picture

Open Interest (OI) is the total number of outstanding (not yet expired or closed) options contracts at a given strike. Unlike volume (which resets each day), OI builds over time and represents the total commitment of money at each strike.

OI LocationMeaningMarket Implication
High CE OI at a strikeLarge number of call writers at this levelResistance — market expected to stay below this strike by expiry
High PE OI at a strikeLarge number of put writers at this levelSupport — market expected to stay above this strike by expiry
Rising CE OI at ATMFresh call writing at current levelBears selling calls = expecting flat/down market
Rising PE OI at ATMFresh put writing at current levelBulls selling puts = expecting flat/up market
📌

OI vs Volume distinction: OI is the total open positions (cumulative). Volume is the number of contracts traded today (resets daily). A strike can have high OI with low volume (old positions held) or low OI with high volume (positions being opened and closed rapidly). For S/R identification, use OI. For momentum signals, use volume changes in OI.

3

Max Pain — The Expiry Magnet

Max Pain is the strike price at which the total dollar value of options expiring worthless is maximised — causing the maximum loss to option buyers as a group. Since option writers (sellers) are typically large institutions and market makers, there is an implicit gravity toward the Max Pain level as expiry approaches.

Max Pain is calculated by summing the total value of all calls and puts that would expire worthless at each possible expiry price, then finding the price at which this total is maximum. Most option chain tools and TradeZen's Options Analysis page calculate this automatically.

Nifty Max Pain — Weekly Expiry Jan 18, 2024
On Tuesday Jan 16, with 2 days to Thursday expiry, Nifty was trading at 22,050. The Max Pain calculation for the expiry showed 22,000 as the pain level. Nifty drifted toward 22,000 through Wednesday and expired at 21,997 — within 3 points of max pain. Option buyers above 22,000 on calls and below 22,000 on puts both experienced losses, while writers collected premiums. This "pinning" to max pain is not coincidence — it is the result of institutional hedging and delta-neutral positioning.
⚠️

Max Pain limitations: Max Pain works best in the last 2–3 days before expiry. Earlier in the week, the market can be far from Max Pain without any gravitational pull. Also, max pain shifts daily as new positions are added — check it fresh each morning near expiry, not just once.

4

PCR — Put-Call Ratio

The PCR (Put-Call Ratio) measures the ratio of total Put OI to total Call OI across all strikes. It's a sentiment indicator — showing whether the overall market is positioned more bullishly (more calls) or bearishly (more puts).

PCR = Total Put OI ÷ Total Call OI

PCR ValueInterpretationContrarian Signal
> 1.3 (very high)Excessive bearishness — too many putsContrarian bullish — market may reverse up
1.0–1.3Moderately bearishNeutral to slightly bullish
0.8–1.0BalancedNeutral — no strong signal
0.5–0.8Moderately bullishNeutral to slightly bearish
< 0.5 (very low)Excessive bullishness — too many callsContrarian bearish — market may reverse down
💡

PCR is a contrarian indicator. When everyone is buying puts (PCR very high), the market is over-hedged bearishly — creating fuel for a bullish reversal as those puts expire worthless. Extreme PCR readings (above 1.5 or below 0.4) at key technical levels are some of the most reliable reversal signals in Nifty.

5

Implied Volatility (IV)

Implied Volatility (IV) is the market's expectation of future volatility embedded in option prices. When IV is high, options are expensive. When IV is low, options are cheap. IV is expressed as a percentage (e.g., Nifty IV = 14% annualised).

IV LevelMeaningStrategy Implication
Low IV (below 12%)Market is calm, options are cheapBuy options (debit strategies). Premium is affordable. Straddles/strangles are cheap to enter.
Normal IV (12–18%)Average market conditionsStandard directional option buying. ATM options offer reasonable premium for expected move.
High IV (above 18%)Market is fearful, options are expensiveSell options (credit strategies). IV crush after the event destroys option values rapidly.
Nifty IV Spike — Budget Day Feb 2024
In the week before the Union Budget (Feb 1, 2024), Nifty ATM IV rose from 11% to 22% as traders bought options expecting a big move. After the Budget announcement, Nifty moved just 200 points. IV crashed from 22% back to 12% within 2 hours — halving the value of ATM options even though price moved. Option buyers who paid inflated premiums lost money despite being directionally correct. This is the "IV crush" phenomenon that destroys option buyers around major events.
6

OI Change Analysis

The change in OI during a session (how much OI was added or reduced) is more informative than the absolute OI level. OI change tells you what's happening in real time.

Bull
Large CE OI being written at a strike while Nifty is below it

Fresh call writing = writers don't expect price to reach that strike by expiry. The strike becomes a strong resistance level. If Nifty approaches it, heavy supply from delta hedging by call writers creates a wall.

Bear
Large CE OI being unwound (reduced) at a resistance strike

When call OI at a resistance level starts declining rapidly, it means call writers are buying back their positions — reducing the "ceiling." This often precedes a breakout through that resistance. Watch for OI reduction as a pre-breakout signal.

Watch
Sharp PE OI addition at a support strike

Fresh put writing at a support level = institutions defending that level, expecting price to stay above it. Strong put addition + price at the level = high-probability bounce setup.

Support & Resistance from Options Chain

The options chain creates dynamic support and resistance levels that update throughout the week. These levels are complementary to the technical S/R, CPR, and EMA levels you've learned — providing an additional dimension from the derivatives market.

1
Identify the highest Call OI strike = resistance

The strike with the most accumulated call OI is where call writers are most committed. This is the strongest overhead resistance from the options market.

2
Identify the highest Put OI strike = support

The strike with the most accumulated put OI is where put writers are most committed. This is the strongest underlying support from the options market.

3
Confluence with technical levels = highest-probability zones

When an options chain support (high PE OI strike) coincides with a technical S/R, CPR level, or EMA, the combined zone is extremely powerful. Both technical and derivatives-based market forces defend the same price.

Options Chain + Technical Confluence — Nifty 22,000 Level 2024
In Jan 2024, the 22,000 strike had the highest put OI on the weekly expiry chain — over 60 lakh lots written. Simultaneously, 22,000 was:
• The prior all-time high (technical resistance → support after breakout)
• A major round-number psychological level
• The CPR BC zone on multiple sessions when Nifty pulled back

Three independent systems (options OI, technical S/R, psychology) all pointed to 22,000 as support. Each time Nifty dipped to 22,000 area, it bounced strongly — the put writers, technical buyers, and psychological buyers all acted simultaneously.

Combining Options Data with TA

The complete intraday analysis framework combines everything from this course into a single decision process:

1
Pre-market: CPR + Options Chain (5 min)

Check today's CPR (TC/BC/PP) from TradeZen Trade Flow. Check the options chain for the highest call and put OI strikes. These define your key levels for the day.

2
Opening: Bias setup from EMA + VWAP + CPR (9:15–9:30)

At 9:15 AM: Is price above or below 20 EMA? Above or below VWAP? Above TC or below BC? Three inputs give immediate bias direction without analysis paralysis.

3
Entry: S/R + Candle + Volume + Options OI confirmation

Wait for price to approach a key level (from all the above). Look for a reversal candle. Check volume is above average. Check if options OI confirms the level (high PE OI = strong support for long). Enter when 3+ confluences align.

4
Risk: Stop below the key level; Target at next resistance

Stop placement is always at the level that invalidates the trade thesis. If you're long at CPR BC (because it's support), the stop goes below BC. Target is TC or the next options chain resistance strike.

Common Mistakes & Key Takeaways

📅
Using Max Pain Too Early in Week
Expecting Nifty to move toward Max Pain on Monday or Tuesday. Max Pain gravity is only relevant in the last 2–3 sessions before expiry.
Fix: Only start referencing Max Pain on Wednesday for Thursday expiry (Nifty weekly). Ignore it Monday and Tuesday.
🔄
Treating PCR as a Direct Signal
Buying when PCR is high (assuming bullish) without understanding it is a CONTRARIAN indicator — high PCR means market is bearishly positioned, which can reverse. It is not a confirmation signal.
Fix: Use PCR as a sentiment extreme detector. Very high PCR (>1.3) at a technical support = contrarian long signal. Not useful in isolation.
💥
Buying Options Before Events (IV Crush)
Buying straddles or ATM options before Budget/RBI policy/Elections when IV is already elevated. Even if right on direction, IV crush destroys the premium after the event.
Fix: Check IV percentile before buying options. If IV is above its 6-month 80th percentile, consider selling strategies instead of buying. Buy options only when IV is low.
🎯
Ignoring OI Shift Before Breakouts
Watching static OI (the total) without tracking daily changes. Missing the signal when large call OI at resistance suddenly starts unwinding — which precedes breakouts.
Fix: Check "OI change today" not just "total OI." A resistance strike with rapidly declining call OI is about to break — that's the pre-breakout signal.
📊
Using Options Chain Without Price Context
Making directional trades based solely on OI data without checking where price is on the technical chart. Options chain gives context — price action gives the timing.
Fix: Always overlay options chain levels on the price chart. Options show WHERE to expect reactions; price action + candles show WHEN to enter.
🔍
Buying Deep OTM Options
Buying far out-of-the-money options because they're cheap (₹5–₹20 premium) expecting a huge move. These options have very low delta — even a 200-point Nifty move may not significantly profit deep OTM options.
Fix: For directional trades, use ATM or 1-strike OTM options. Deep OTM options are for defined-risk hedges, not core directional positions.
